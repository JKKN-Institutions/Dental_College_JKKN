## Section 2: Step-by-Step — Filing Google AI Feedback

### 2.1 Where to Find the Feedback Button

The feedback button on an AI Overview is small and easy to miss. Here is exactly where it is:

1. Search your query on Google
2. Scroll up to see the AI Overview box (blue or purple bordered box at the top)
3. Look at the **bottom-right corner** of the AI Overview box
4. You will see a small **thumbs down icon** (👎) or a **three-dot menu icon** (⋮)
5. Next to the thumbs icons, there is also a **"More" or feedback link** — this varies by device

**On Mobile:** The feedback option is under the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner of the AI Overview card.

### 2.2 Exact Steps to File Feedback

**Step 1:** Open Chrome in Incognito mode and search `JKKN Dental College review`

**Step 2:** Locate the AI Overview box at the top of the search results

**Step 3:** Look for the thumbs down icon (👎) at the bottom right of the AI Overview box and click it

**Step 4:** A feedback panel will appear. You will see options like:
- Not helpful
- Inaccurate
- Offensive or unsafe
- Other

**Step 5:** Select **"Inaccurate"** — this is the most appropriate category for fabricated factual claims

**Step 6:** After selecting "Inaccurate", a text box will appear asking for more details. This is where you paste the feedback template (see Section 3)

**Step 7:** Click **"Submit"** or **"Send Feedback"**

**Step 8:** Take a screenshot of the confirmation screen (it will say something like "Thanks for your feedback")

**Step 9:** Note the date and time in your documentation log

**Step 10:** Repeat this process from a different browser and device (minimum 3 separate feedback submissions recommended)

### 2.3 Alternative Feedback Path: Google Help Center

If the thumbs-down button is not visible:

1. Go to: **https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/9281931**
2. Scroll to the section "Send feedback about AI Overviews"
3. Click the feedback link in that section
4. Fill in the form with the template from Section 3

### 2.4 Additional Reporting Channel: Google Search Quality Feedback

1. Go to: **https://www.google.com/contact/aboutsearch.html**
2. Select "Report an issue with Search"
3. Describe the problem using the template from Section 3

## Section 5: Parallel Actions to Dilute the Negative Signal

These actions address the root cause — the data imbalance — rather than just the symptom. Do all of these simultaneously with the feedback filing.

### 5.1 Get More Positive Reviews on Shiksha (Target: 50+ Reviews)

This is the highest priority parallel action. When Shiksha has 50+ reviews, the single negative review loses its statistical weight and the AI is less likely to treat it as representative.

**Who to ask:**
- Final year BDS students (they have 4 years of experience to write about)
- Interns currently doing clinical rotations
- Recently graduated alumni (2022, 2023, 2024 batches)
- Postgraduate MDS students

**How to ask:**
1. Send a WhatsApp message to the final year and intern batch WhatsApp groups
2. Message text (customize as needed):
   > "Namba college Shiksha la only 2 reviews iruku. One negative review iruku, which is affecting our college's AI search results. Please help by writing a genuine positive review about your experience — especially about the dental chair facilities, OPG machines, and clinical infrastructure. Link: https://www.shiksha.com/[JKKN-Dental-page-URL]"
3. Do NOT ask for fake reviews — ask for genuine, specific reviews from real students
4. Remind students to mention specific details: chair quality, equipment availability, OPG usage in clinics, patient flow, faculty supervision

**What reviewers should include (suggest, do not dictate):**
- Specific mention of dental chair quality and hygiene standards
- Their experience using OPG machines
- Patient volume and clinical exposure
- Infrastructure and equipment availability

### 5.2 Complete Profiles on Collegedunia and Careers360

Google's AI also sources from these platforms. If these profiles are incomplete or missing, the AI has fewer authoritative sources to balance against the Shiksha review.

**Collegedunia:**
1. Go to: https://collegedunia.com/
2. Search for JKKN Dental College — claim the listing or contact them at support@collegedunia.com
3. Complete all fields: facilities, fee structure, placements, faculty count, clinical stats
4. Upload photos of dental labs, OPG machines, and clinical areas
5. Request verified reviews from students through their official review program

**Careers360:**
1. Go to: https://careers360.com/
2. Search for JKKN Dental College — claim and complete the profile
3. Fill in NAAC grade, affiliations, infrastructure details, placement data
4. Upload accreditation certificates and NAAC documents as proof

### 5.3 Publish Authoritative Content That Directly Counters the Claims

Create content on the official website (dental.jkkn.ac.in) that specifically addresses infrastructure quality. Search engines and AI systems will index this content and eventually use it as a counterweight.

**Recommended pages to create:**
1. `/infrastructure/` — A dedicated infrastructure page with photos and statistics
   - Title: "Dental Infrastructure at JKKN Dental College — 200 Chairs, Modern Equipment"
   - Include: chair count, OPG machine model and count, sterilization protocols, annual maintenance records
   - Photos: dental operatories, OPG room, equipment labels visible

2. `/clinical-facilities/` — Clinical exposure page
   - Title: "Clinical Training Facilities — 500+ Daily Patients, Full Equipment Access"
   - Include: patient count per day, chair availability ratio per student, equipment usage in curriculum

3. A blog post: "A Day in the Clinical Department — JKKN Dental College"
   - First-person style, describes student experience using dental chairs, OPG, clinical workflow
   - Published under an actual faculty or student name with credentials

### 5.4 Encourage Detailed Reviews That Specifically Counter the Fabricated Claims

When asking students for reviews on Google Maps, Justdial, or Shiksha, suggest (do not mandate) that they mention:
- Dental chair quality and cleanliness
- Availability and condition of OPG machines
- Water supply and hygiene at clinical stations
- Equipment access and faculty supervision

This ensures that when AI systems read future reviews, they encounter specific factual statements about the exact topics the negative review raised.

## Section 7: Legal Escalation Path (If Feedback Does Not Work)

Use this section only after 6–8 weeks of feedback filing with no change in the AI Overview.

### 7.1 When to Consider Legal Notice

Consider legal escalation if ALL of the following are true:
- You have filed Google feedback at least 4 times over 6 weeks
- The fabricated claims remain unchanged in the AI Overview
- You can document that prospective students or parents have referenced these claims as a reason for not applying
- The claims remain unverified and no credible source is found to support them

### 7.2 Steps Before Sending Legal Notice

1. **Compile your evidence file:**
   - All screenshots of AI Overview (with dates)
   - Your monitoring log showing persistence over 6+ weeks
   - Documentation of all feedback submissions (with confirmation screenshots)
   - Verified ratings from Google Maps, Justdial, Shiksha, Facebook (with URLs and screenshot dates)
   - Statement from institution confirming OPG machines are operational and water supply is functional
   - If possible: photos of operational OPG machines with date metadata

2. **Consult a legal professional** before sending any notice — specifically one with experience in technology law or defamation in India

3. **Identify the correct legal entity:** Google LLC (for US-based legal matters) or Google India Private Limited (for India-based notices under IT Act 2000)

### 7.4 Alternative: Counter-Content Strategy (Non-Legal Escalation)

If legal action is disproportionate or premature, this is the most effective long-term alternative:

| Action | Goal | Timeline |
|--------|------|----------|
| Publish 5+ authoritative pages about dental infrastructure | Give AI alternative credible sources | 2–4 weeks |
| Get 50+ Shiksha reviews | Statistically drown out the single negative review | 4–8 weeks |
| Submit institution data to knowledge panels (Wikidata, Schema.org) | Establish authoritative structured data | 2–3 weeks |
| Create a YouTube video tour of dental labs and OPG rooms | AI systems use video content as signals | 1–2 weeks |
| Get press coverage in Tamil education or medical news | High-authority third-party source | 4–8 weeks |

The counter-content strategy is lower risk, lower cost, and often more effective than legal action because it addresses the underlying data problem rather than just the symptom.
